I am a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University. My approach to teaching emphasizes clear, precise explanations of deep technical material without sacrificing rigor. I treat students as peers engaged in the same intellectual work, and I structure courses around hands-on environments where students interact with real systems rather than abstractions. The goal is to develop both theoretical understanding and practical instinct — students should leave a course able to reason about systems they have never seen before.

I received the Computer Science Department Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award in 2014.
