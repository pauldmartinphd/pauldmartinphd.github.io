Hardware Analysis Laboratory

I maintain a dedicated hardware analysis laboratory equipped for the component-level investigation, firmware extraction, and forensic analysis that my expert witness and research work requires. The lab includes oscilloscopes, logic analyzers, stereo microscopes, hot-air and infrared rework stations, soldering stations, programmable power supplies, and a range of device-specific diagnostic tools.

This equipment supports board-level fault isolation, firmware dumping and analysis, signal capture and protocol decoding, and component-level repair — capabilities that are directly applied in litigation involving hardware failures, embedded system disputes, and firmware forensics. Outside of professional work, I have performed component-level repair, firmware modification, and low-level hardware analysis across dozens of platforms — including BIOS and ECU firmware flashing, GPU cross-flashing, PLL overclocking, CPU pin modification, and custom firmware installation on locked-down consumer devices.
